Poster Playground

TIMELINE

January 2022

1 week

TOOLS

Figma

TASK

Using the provided text and at least one image from the provided folder,

create a 1024px by 1600px poster.

PROJECT SUMMARY

This exercise was aimed to build skills in visual design. When creating my posters, I worked with font choice, color scheme, images, layout, information hierarchy, and more to explore communication within artistry. Given the constraints of textual density and selected photos, I worked iteratively to produce unique pieces.

POSTER 1

My main focus of this version was how I can manipulate the typography to create a visually interesting element on its own. As I played around with moving letters and blocks of text, I found myself creating a scene that resembled a museum display.

POSTER 2

I started with the photo for this poster. Because it reminded me of a science museum, I was inspired to format the poster like a science textbook. I went with an earth-themed color scheme drawn from the image.

POSTER 3

Because I was dealing with a lot of text, I began this process by constructing the typography. I then checked what images might fit in the space on the side and found this futuristic building. With limited room on the side, I had the idea to split the image and put one half on each of the sides. The black background with the white buildings created a bold look which I further amplified with the addition of orange text for pops of color.

POSTER 4

I wanted to challenge myself by continuing to try a different aesthetic for every poster. While I had used images of buildings, I had not yet touched images of people. The original photo of this individual was quite formal, but through tracing and cropping, I made the image more mysterious and playful. Unsure of what to do next, I mindless zoomed in and out until I realized I could use the patches of pink skin as inspiration. I created oval shapes that mimicked what I had seen in the image and color dropped from those sections. I placed these spots around the canvas offsetting the supplemental information and therefore distinguishing it from the program information. I sampled my contrast color from the person's eyes.
